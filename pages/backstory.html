Backstory

I’m a career IT Systems Analyst. I use that title loosely to refer to the various systems roles I’ve filled over the years, be they “tech support”, “system administrator”, “system engineer”, etc. I work primarily with Unix and Linux systems.

In 2008 I began taking courses part-time at Queen’s University, and I continue to slowly chip away at a B.A. in Economics with a side order of Computing and Mathematics / Statistics. Perhaps I might extend my studies to a B.A.H. with a minor in a related field, but the cost-benefit analysis is still out on that.

Recently I expressed to my management an interest in taking on new challenges, possibly even a new role within the company. It was suggested that I might get more closely involved with our nascent data team. A smashing suggestion that leverages much of my existing IT systems experience (“data engineering”), my technical / analytic soft skills (root-cause analysis wizard), and my University studies (Econometrics / Statistics / Computing)..

So I began devouring data related books and MOOCs. My collection of O’Reilly e-books has ballooned. I’m about one half of the way through the Johns Hopkins Data Science Specialization from Coursera, and I am currently working through the 2-month University of Washington “Introduction to Data Science”, also via Coursera.

Claudia Gold’s “Data Analysis” is proving to be a valuable guidebook on the road to mad data skills. This past Q1, while taking “Introduction to Mathematical Economics” at Queen’s, I also took the MITx MOOC “6.00.1x Introduction to Computer Science and Programming using Python”. My background (first kick at the University can) is actually in Computing so this course prooved to be pretty easy, but it was a great way to jumpstart my python skills, being a long time perl hack.

R programming on DataCamp, probability and stats at Khan Academy, data anlysis and programming courses via Udacity. The list goes on and I continue to devote considerable free time to my data skills development. This Fall I will be taking the “Introductory Econometrics” course at Queen’s, both relevent to my efforts and another half course toward my B.A. Linear Algebra, Data Mining and Database courses are in the queue.
