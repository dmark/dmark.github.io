In general I really don't want friends and family to buy me stuff. I have more stuff than I know what to do with, I am annoyingly particular, and my hobbies are pretty expensive...

  • Which is why the perfect gift is: a curry from Cambodian Village or any of the other great Thai / Cambodian / Vietnamese places around town. Or Indian. It's consumable, it's my favourite food, and something I don't normally buy myself.
  • No booze or junk food please, except maybe a small bottle of Magnum or Bailey's for Christmas coffee. Spiked Christmas coffee is the one alcoholic treat I will allow myself. And I'll never say no to a milk chocolate Ritter Sport bar.
  • Fountain pen ink. I like collecting fountain pen inks. I definitely don't need any more ink as I am certain I have enough to last the rest of my life, but I'll never say no to more! Just make sure it is fountain pen ink and not calligraphy or India ink. wonderpens.ca is a good place for this. phidonpens.com is good too but their prices tend to be high. Some suggestions follow but feel free to pick something else that is fun since I tend to buy more "masculine" colours. I also don't have much in the way greys / silvers:
    • Herbin Stormy Grey
    • Diamine Silver Fox
    • Diamine Moon Dust
    • Noodler's Zhivago
    • Diamine Writer's Blood
    • Ferris Wheel Press Moss Park Green
  • Other fountain pen fanboi stuff. Quality notebooks (Clairefontaine, Rhodia, etc.) are always good as I do most of my note-taking with pen and paper. I mostly use dotted hardcover notebooks in A5 size. Softcover and grid are probably ok too!
  • The Fractal Design Define 7 Compact computer chassis, black, without the tempered glass side panel. This is a bit silly because I have the Fractal Design Meshify C chassis which is basically the same as the Define. I just prefer the look of the Define over the Meshify.
  • The #0, #1, and #2 bands from Rogue available here

And if you're feeling flush and want to throw down on a Pelikan Souveran M1000 or a Parker Duofold 100th Anniversary Edition, who am I to deny such generosity?
