Requirements

Self-motivated, quick learner, fast researcher, and hungry to improve technical skills.

I love digging into new technology, and like many technologists, this is both a vocation, and a hobby. I frequently take a leadership position in the adoption of new technology, including leading my current employer's adoption of cloud computing platforms and services such as Auth0, Google Cloud Platform, and Amazon Web Services.

Love to code and solve technical issues varying in complexity and technology.

I enjoy solving technical problems and, while not a professional software engineer, I love to solving such problems with code. A succinct bit of PEP8 compliant python code is a joy to behold! I have been making an explicit effort to build out a bit of a public github portfolio (most of my past code belonging to my employer and not open-source).

Good at empathizing with developers and have experience with troubleshooting environments.

While my role has always involved supporting developers, for the past two and half years I have been embedded with our web application / "digital transformation" product development team, providing them with operational "SRE" support. "My developers" have come to rely on me as someone who can help resolve their technical challenges.

Excellent written and verbal communication skills (English) and passionate about talking to developers.

See the next bullet.

Enjoy writing and teaching, capable of explaining complex concepts in easy-to-understand language.

I have often been in the position of teaching / communicating complex technology to others, and many times I have been commended on my ability to explain complex topics in a way that makes it easy for other to understand. My public speaking skills are likely a bit rusty, but I used to give public presentations on technically deep subject matter to my local Linux Users Group (q.v.) every month or two.

Have experience with dealing with a forum and are comfortable with a remote working environment.

Outside the Auth0 Community, my forum experience is limited. I participate in many forums (stackoverflow, motorcycle forums) but it hasn't been something I engage in frequently.
