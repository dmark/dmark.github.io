Attention

By: Mark Drummond On: Wed 20 August 2025
In: Posts
Tags: #time-management

I'm a big fan of Oliver Burkeman's take on time and work management.

Summarizing, and likely butchering, part of Burkeman's thesis, your life comprises entirely, and only, that which you have directed your attention toward. Don't think in terms of your todo list. Think instead in terms of everything that competes for your attention.

When I expand the scope of my "todo" list to "that which is competing for my attention", the result is alarming. At the time of this writing, my list is nearing 180 line items, and growing with every meeting-filled day.

Speaking of meetings, I have not yet included in my list meetings, and in particular recurring meetings. Every meeting requires some attention before you start the meeting. The meeting commands your attention for its duration. And the result of the meeting is likely one or more new items demanding yet more attention.

Try making a list of everything competing for your attention.
