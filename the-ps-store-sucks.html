The PlayStation Store Sucks

Posted on Wed 19 July 2017 in Rants

I’ve played a ton of great games on my PlayStation consoles over the years, and I have a decent sized backlog of more great games yet to play. And of course, Destiny 2 is just around the corner. Which brings me to this …

Yes, the PS Store design is something straight out of 10 years ago, but that is not what I am refering to. For years now the Store has been locking out paying customers for reasons that boggle the mind. The usual case is, at the final step in the check out process, the user gets a completely useless “there was an error” type error message:

Note in particular the super helpful “Please try again later”. That’s more important than you might realise. Sure, there’s the obvious ambiguity … just what the hell does Sony mean by “later”? But there more to it than that. And keep in mind this error seems to strike randomly. There’s nothing that the user does that causes this to happen.

This past week I was once again plagued by this error message while I was trying to buy Destiny 2. As usual I went to my console. Only this time, my console gave me an error as well, telling me my credit card information was invalid. Ok, but I’m paying with PayPal sooooo… I tried making my purchase from my laptop (Linux Mint 18.2 with Firefox and Chromium, from my Windows 7 VM with Firefox, from one of my tablets, and my PS4 Pro. No dice.

After a chat session with support and some DuckDuckGo-fu, it turns out the reason this happens is one of the most bat-shit crazy IT fuck ups I can recall. The root cause? If there’s an error communicating with a payment provider during the checkout process, the PS Store’s fraud detection systems will lock you “for security reasons”. The lock out lasts for 24 hours, and to make matters worse, the clock is reset every time you try checking out within the 24 hour window. Note that the error messages tell you none of this. A customer can keep themselves locked out indefinitely!

Let’s just repeat that so it’s clear: When the PS Store has a problem communicating with a payment provider, the Store locks the user out! This has nothing to do with protecting you from fruadulent activities. This is the PS Store talking to PayPal or your credit card company. This problem goes back YEARS, and Sony has made no effort whatsoever to fix it!

Just imagine if Amazon locked you out every time their systems had a problem communicating with a payment provider!
